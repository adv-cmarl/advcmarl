On the Robustness of Cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

  • Learn more

Abstract

In cooperative multi-agent reinforcement learning (c-MARL), agents learn to cooperatively take actions to maximize a total team reward. We analyze the robustness of c-MARL to adversaries capable of attacking one of the agents by manipulating its observations. Attacking c-MARL is challenging because (1) it is difficult to estimate team rewards or how they are impacted by an agent mispredicting; (2) feature space is often low-dimensional with mixed-integer values. Thus, we introduce a novel attack, and empirically validate its effectiveness on multiple c-MARL training algorithms. Our results on the StarCraft II multi-agent benchmark show c-MARL teams are highly vulnerable to perturbations applied to agent observations. By attacking a single agent, our attack has highly negative impact on the team reward, consequently reducing the team's win rate by up to 100%.
